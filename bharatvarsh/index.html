What is Sanatan?


Sanatan, derived from Sanskrit, means eternal or timeless. It signifies the ancient, unchanging principles of Hinduism, encompassing spiritual, moral, and social guidelines. Sanatan Dharma, often referred to as Hinduism, emphasizes the cyclical nature of life, karma, and dharma. It embodies enduring values and practices, forming the core of Hindu religious and philosophical beliefs.

What is Bharatvarsh?


Bharatvarsh, also known as Bharata or Bharat, is the ancient Sanskrit name for the Indian subcontinent. It holds significant cultural and historical importance in Hindu tradition and literature. In Hindu mythology, it's believed to be named after King Bharata, an ancestor of the epic hero Rama. Today, Bharatvarsh refers to the Republic of India, encompassing its diverse cultures, languages, and traditions.

What makes Sanatan unique compared to other religions?


Hinduism stands out for its diversity, encompassing a vast array of beliefs, practices, and rituals. Unlike many religions, it lacks a single founder and promotes tolerance for various interpretations of spirituality. Its emphasis on karma, dharma, and moksha, along with concepts like reincarnation, distinguishes it from other major belief systems.

What is Dharma?


Dharma, refers to the moral and ethical duties and righteousness that individuals must follow to lead a virtuous life. It encompasses one's responsibilities towards society, family, and oneself, as outlined in sacred texts like the Vedas and Upanishads. Dharma is a fundamental concept shaping Hindu ethics and spirituality.
